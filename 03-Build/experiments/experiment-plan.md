# Experiment Plan

**Team:** Nemesis  
**Product:** Musa - Craft Shop Marketplace  
**Date launched:** 21 May 2026  
**Owner:** Ketevan Shavadze  

---

## 1. Hypothesis

We believe handmade creators in Georgia who currently sell or try to sell through Instagram, Facebook, or direct messages will sign up for Musa’s early seller access at a rate of **18% or more** because they experience real friction with pricing, product organization, trust-building, and managing orders through scattered messages.

---

## 2. Assumption Being Tested

Handmade creators do not only complain about selling being difficult — they care enough to take a concrete action before the full marketplace exists.

The action we are testing is whether they will give contact information, describe their craft, and submit at least one product photo or listing idea for early seller access. This is stronger than simply saying “I like the idea” in an interview.

If creators are not willing to take this small step now, they may also avoid creating listings after launch.

---

## 3. Top 3 Riskiest Assumptions

| Rank | Assumption | Why risky | Why this experiment addresses it |
|------|------------|----------|----------------------------------|
| 1 | Handmade creators will take action when offered a simpler selling workflow | Interviews showed pain around pricing, DMs, visibility, and trust, but interview pain does not always turn into product adoption | The experiment asks creators to sign up and submit real product information before the platform is fully built |
| 2 | The value proposition is clear without a long explanation | Creators may think Musa is just another marketplace or Instagram page unless the benefit is immediately understandable | A one-page landing page tests whether the message “create a clean shop instead of selling through messy DMs” is clear enough |
| 3 | Creators can be reached through online craft/community channels | If signups only come from personal friends, the launch channel will be too weak | Sharing through Instagram craft pages, student creator networks, and local handmade groups tests whether Musa can reach creators beyond the team’s circle |

---

## 4. Experiment Method

**Method chosen:** Smoke test + concierge seller onboarding

A one-page landing page is published before the full marketplace is launched. The page explains Musa’s core seller value proposition, shows a mockup of the creator shop / listing flow, and includes one main call to action: **“Join early seller access.”**

The form asks creators to provide their name, contact information, craft category, current selling channel, and one product photo or product idea. This tests whether creators are willing to start the seller onboarding process.

No full product is required yet. If a creator signs up, the team manually follows up and helps them prepare their first listing. This simulates the future onboarding experience while keeping the experiment lightweight.

- **Channel:** Instagram craft pages, Facebook handmade groups, KIU/student creator networks, direct messages to creators who already post handmade products
- **Asset used:** Landing page with one headline, one product mockup, short explanation, and Google Form / Typeform signup
- **Main headline:** “Turn your handmade products into a clean online shop — without managing everything through DMs.”
- **Call to action:** “Join early seller access”
- **Target users reached by:** sharing the page with people who already make handmade items such as jewelry, crochet, candles, accessories, art, or custom gifts
- **What happens after a creator responds:** their response is saved in a tracking sheet; the team contacts them and helps prepare their first product listing manually

---

## 5. Success, Gray Zone, Failure

These thresholds are decided before launch and should not be changed after seeing the results.

- **Success threshold:** 18% or more of unique landing page visitors complete the seller signup form
- **Strong success signal:** At least 10 creators submit a product photo or specific listing idea
- **Gray zone:** 8% to 17% signup rate — there is some demand, but the message, audience, or onboarding ask may need improvement
- **Failure threshold:** Below 8% signup rate — creators may like the idea in theory but are not motivated enough to act; revisit the value proposition before building more marketplace features

The main success metric is not just page visits. The important action is:

**Creator activation proxy = completed seller form + product category + product photo/listing idea**

---

## 6. Time Window and Sample Size

- **Experiment starts:** 21 May 2026
- **Experiment ends:** 28 May 2026
- **Minimum sample target:** 60 unique visitors from real handmade creators or people interested in selling handmade products
- **Minimum valid creator responses:** 10 completed seller signup forms
- **What counts as one valid data point:** one unique visitor who arrives from a craft-related channel and is not a team member, close friend, or family member signing up only to help the project

The experiment should run for one week so there is enough time to test different channels without changing the core landing page message.

## 9. Decision Rule

- **If result meets success threshold (≥18%):** continue building the seller onboarding flow, product listing creation, and creator studio features. This confirms that creators are willing to move beyond Instagram/DM selling and try a structured marketplace.

- **If result is in the gray zone (8%–17%):** do not immediately abandon the idea. Run a second experiment with improved copy or a more specific niche, such as “handmade jewelry sellers” or “crochet creators.” Also test whether offering free first-listing setup increases conversion.

- **If result falls below failure threshold (<8%):** pause the seller-side build and re-check the problem. The issue may be that creators do not trust a new marketplace yet, do not understand the value, or are not ready to list products publicly. Run follow-up interviews before building more marketplace features.

---

## 10. What Would Make This Experiment Invalid

- More than 30% of signups come from team members, family, or close friends who are signing up only to support the project
- The landing page copy changes during the experiment without recording the old version, new version, and date of change
- The audience is too broad, for example general students instead of people who actually make or want to sell handmade products
- Fewer than 60 unique visitors are reached by 28 May 2026
- The form does not ask for a real seller action, such as product category, product idea, or product photo
- Most traffic comes from direct personal messages instead of craft-related channels
- The team counts “likes” or positive comments as success instead of completed seller signup actions

---

## 11. Main Learning Goal

This experiment is not only testing whether people like the idea of Musa. It is testing whether handmade creators are willing to begin seller onboarding before the full product exists.

The strongest signal is a creator saying, through action:

“I want a cleaner way to sell my handmade products, and I am ready to prepare my first listing.”
